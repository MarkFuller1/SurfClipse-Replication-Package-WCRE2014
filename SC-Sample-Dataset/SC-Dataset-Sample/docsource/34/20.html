  • java.net.SocketException: Socket is closed

Often because of one's own initiative to close the connection (called a Socket's close approach) and then read and write operations on the network connection.

  • java.net.SocketException: (Connection reset or Connect reset by peer: Socket write error)

The first is that if one end of the Socket is closed (or take the initiative to shut down or quit because of abnormalities caused by the closure), the other side is still sending data, send the first packet trigger the exception (Connect reset by peer). Another is the end of the exit, but did not close the connection when out of the other side if the data is read from the connection throws the exception (Connection reset). Simply put, that is disconnected after the read and write operations caused.
