up vote 0 down vote

your receiverArray is initialized with the size of the queue, but then the queue expands while you read it, and you end up with more data than you expected.

You have to make sure you are not reading too much, most likely by testing the index against the size (the extra data would be read at the next execution of readDataFromReceiverBuffer)

You could also assign a much larger array (but testing is still mandatory)

Your second issue could be about your task to be too long, and therefore some overlaping and concurrent access to the queue. (basically, 2 instances test if the queue is empty, both try to read from it, but there is only one element)

I would use a thread with a loop, a time measurement of the loop execution, and a sleep for delaying until the next required execution, rather than a timer. That would ensure single access to reading the queue.
