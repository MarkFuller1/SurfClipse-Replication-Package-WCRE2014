I think many people quit because it's just hard! Making good food choices for every meal, six days a week is hard. Even though most folks on this site give themselves a free day, when you see your family, your friends and your co-workers eating yummy crap that tastes so good, it can be hard to say "no, thank you" all the time.

Committing yourself to working out is hard! When it's 34 degrees outside and spitting icy cold rain, and the rest of your family is curled up on the couch in their snuggies, and you are heading out the door in the freezing cold to get to the gym, it's a much easier choice to put on a snuggie of your own, dive into the popcorn, and veg out.

Even if you have a great place to work out in your own home, choosing to work out is hard! You get up at 5 a.m. when everyone else is still sleeping, wipe the crust out of your eyes and take a long walk to the basement to work out, knowing that as soon as you are done, you need to get the kids up, get them ready and head them off to school, iron your clothes for work, see your spouse out the door, then head off to work yourself for an 8-hour day (or more!)

If you wait and exercise after work, it's not any easier. You come in the door, your spouse is hungry, the kids are wanting to tell you about their day, (plus they are hungry,) there are 3 loads of laundry than need to be washed, dried and put away, the dog needs attention, the cat wants you to acknowledge her magnificence, and the floors need to be swept because the nasty weather has once again left muddy footprints all across your wood floor. Once you sweep, you see it's not enough, so you really need to do a once-over with a mop.

Once you mop and feed the human and animal population, it's bath time for kiddos! Then story, then bed. What if spouse wants a little bouncy woo-hoo time? Well, that is when you have a choice to make: Do you use your last reserve of energy for bouncy woo-hoo time so spousal unit will be happy, or do you get in that 9 p.m. workout, knowing in the back of your head that denying the call of woo-hoo will lead to guilty feelings and a frustrated spouse?

So....why do I think people stop working out? Because making the choice and the commitment to yourself, the choice to find some little chunk of day just for you is very, very hard. Eating well when everyone around you is not, isn't easy.

People who carve out that little space, who do it day after day, year after year, those folks are to be admired (and emulated!)

Also, once you start, keep on going. Stopping is WAY easier than keeping on.
